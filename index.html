About

My path: psychology → analytics → behavioural data science

My academic interest started with a simple question: why do people behave the way they do? Over time, I realized I’m especially interested in questions that require data to answer — how decisions form under complexity, how choice environments shape behaviour, and how small design cues can reduce cognitive burden.

A key turning point was designing and running studies end-to-end, including survey/experimental design, data cleaning, and analysis. I enjoy combining behavioural theory with tools like SPSS, R, Python, and SQL to produce results that are both meaningful and actionable.

Choice overload & decision fatigue Psychometrics (EFA, reliability) Behaviour change & persuasion Clear reporting & visualization
